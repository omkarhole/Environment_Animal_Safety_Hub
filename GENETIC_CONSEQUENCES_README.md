# Genetic Consequences of Captive Breeding Programs - Issue #1595

## Overview

This educational feature explores the genetic challenges and consequences of captive breeding programs in wildlife conservation. Captive breeding, while essential for preventing extinction, can introduce significant genetic issues including inbreeding depression, domestication selection, and reduced post-release survival rates.

## Issue Reference
- **GitHub Issue**: #1595
- **Title**: Genetic Consequences of Captive Breeding Programs
- **Description**: Examine inbreeding, domestication selection, and post-release survival in conservation breeding efforts

## Educational Objectives

1. **Understand Genetic Principles in Captive Breeding**
   - Learn about genetic diversity and heterozygosity
   - Recognize inbreeding depression mechanisms
   - Understand domestication selection pressures

2. **Explore Conservation Breeding Challenges**
   - Examine founder effects and genetic bottlenecks
   - Study artificial selection in captivity
   - Analyze genetic adaptation to captive environments

3. **Investigate Post-Release Consequences**
   - Review survival rates of captive-bred individuals
   - Understand behavioral deficiencies
   - Examine genetic fitness in wild populations

4. **Examine Mitigation Strategies**
   - Learn about genetic management techniques
   - Understand population viability analysis
   - Explore assisted gene flow and genetic rescue
