# Plastic Nanoparticles and Cellular Toxicity

## Issue #1606 Implementation

### Overview
This educational feature explores how plastic nanoparticles (nanoplastics) penetrate biological tissues and disrupt cellular processes in aquatic and terrestrial organisms, examining the mechanisms of toxicity at the cellular and subcellular levels.

### Educational Objectives
- Understand the sources and characteristics of plastic nanoparticles
- Learn how nanoplastics penetrate biological barriers and tissues
- Explore cellular toxicity mechanisms and oxidative stress pathways
- Examine the cascading effects on organism health and ecosystem function
- Investigate detection methods and potential mitigation strategies

### Scientific Foundation

#### Key Concepts
- **Nanoplastic Characteristics**: Size (1-100 nm), surface charge, chemical composition, aggregation behavior
- **Cellular Interactions**: Protein corona formation, membrane interactions, intracellular trafficking
- **Toxicity Mechanisms**: Physical damage, chemical toxicity, biological responses
- **Bioaccumulation**: Trophic transfer and biomagnification in food chains
- **Environmental Fate**: Transport, transformation, and persistence in ecosystems

#### Nanoparticle Types
- **Primary Nanoplastics**: Manufactured nanoparticles (cosmetics, textiles, industrial)
- **Secondary Nanoplastics**: Degradation products of larger plastics (UV, mechanical, biological breakdown)
- **Surface Modifications**: Pristine vs. weathered, functionalized vs. unmodified
- **Polymer Types**: PE, PP, PS, PVC with different toxicity profiles

#### Cellular Targets
- **Cell Membrane**: Integrity disruption, permeability changes, receptor interactions
- **Mitochondria**: Energy production impairment, ROS generation, apoptosis induction
- **Lysosomes**: Accumulation and dysfunction, autophagy disruption
- **Nucleus**: DNA damage, gene expression changes, chromatin modifications
- **Endoplasmic Reticulum**: Protein folding stress, calcium homeostasis disruption

#### Organ Systems Affected
- **Digestive System**: Gut microbiome disruption, nutrient absorption impairment
- **Respiratory System**: Gill damage in fish, lung inflammation in terrestrial organisms
- **Nervous System**: Neurotoxicity, behavioral changes, neurotransmitter disruption
- **Reproductive System**: Endocrine disruption, gamete quality reduction
- **Immune System**: Immunotoxicity, inflammation, disease susceptibility
