Genetic Challenges in Captive Breeding

Understanding the genetic mechanisms that threaten captive populations

Founder Effects

Limited genetic diversity from small founding populations leads to loss of alleles and reduced adaptability.

Genetic Drift

Random changes in allele frequencies, especially severe in small populations, can lead to fixation of harmful alleles.

Inbreeding Depression

Reduced fitness due to increased homozygosity of deleterious recessive alleles affects survival and reproduction.

Domestication Selection

Unintentional artificial selection in captivity favors traits suited to captive environments over wild survival skills.

Conservation Breeding Case Studies

Real-world examples of genetic challenges and solutions

California Condor

Extreme inbreeding depression led to developmental abnormalities and low fertility. Genetic rescue through cross-fostering saved the species.

Black-footed Ferret

Population bottleneck reduced genetic diversity to critical levels. Reintroduction programs face domestication selection challenges.

Przewalski's Horse

Successful reintroduction but captive-bred individuals show reduced wild survival skills and altered social behaviors.

Takahe

New Zealand flightless bird shows behavioral changes in captivity that affect post-release survival and breeding success.
