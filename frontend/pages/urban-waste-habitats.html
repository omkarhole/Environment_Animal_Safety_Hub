Fresh Kills Landfill Case Study

Location: Staten Island, New York

Former world's largest landfill, closed in 2001

Key Findings:

  • Supports over 200 bird species during migration
  • Heavy metal contamination in soil and groundwater
  • Ongoing rehabilitation efforts to reduce ecological trap effects
  • Population booms in opportunistic species followed by crashes

Current Status:

The site is undergoing transformation into Freshkills Park, with careful management to balance wildlife habitat creation and contamination remediation.

×

Bordo Poniente Landfill Case Study

Location: Mexico City, Mexico

Latin America's largest landfill

Key Findings:

  • Home to significant populations of coyotes, foxes, and feral dogs
  • High disease transmission rates among mammalian colonizers
  • Artificial population explosions due to abundant food resources
  • Conflicts with nearby human communities

Current Status:

Implementing integrated waste management and wildlife monitoring programs to mitigate ecological trap effects.

×

Laogang Landfill Case Study

Location: Shanghai, China

Modern waste-to-energy facility

Key Findings:

  • Diverse insect communities driving waste decomposition
  • Successful bioremediation through microbial activity
  • Controlled habitat creation for beneficial decomposers
  • Reduced methane emissions through active management

Current Status:

Serves as a model for sustainable waste management with positive ecological outcomes.

×

Deonar Dumping Ground Case Study

Location: Mumbai, India

Primary waste disposal site for metropolitan area

Key Findings:

  • High density of urban-adapted species (dogs, monkeys, birds)
  • Significant human-wildlife conflict incidents
  • Rapid behavioral adaptations to urban waste environments
  • Public health concerns from disease transmission

Current Status:

Implementing community education and waste management improvements to reduce ecological trap effects.
