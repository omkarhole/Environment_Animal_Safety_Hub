Nutrient Cycling

Microorganisms drive the cycling of essential nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and carbon, making them available for plants and animals.

Decomposition

Bacteria and fungi break down organic matter, recycling nutrients and maintaining soil fertility and water quality.

Pathogen Control

Beneficial microbes compete with harmful pathogens, protecting plants, animals, and humans from disease.

Plant Symbiosis

Mycorrhizal fungi and nitrogen-fixing bacteria form essential partnerships with plants for nutrient uptake.

Remediation Strategies

Bioremediation

Using microorganisms to degrade pollutants and restore ecosystem functions.

Bioaugmentation

Adding specific microbial strains to enhance pollutant degradation.

Phytoremediation

Using plants and their associated microbes to remove contaminants.

Natural Attenuation

Allowing natural microbial processes to reduce pollutant concentrations over time.
