Underwater Noise Crisis

Shipping traffic generates chronic low-frequency noise that travels thousands of kilometers underwater, disrupting marine mammal communication and behavior.

Noise Frequency Spectrum

Communication Disruption

Marine mammals rely on sound for navigation, foraging, and social bonding. Shipping noise masks these vital acoustic signals.

Migration Route Changes

Chronic noise pollution forces marine mammals to alter their traditional migration patterns, increasing energy expenditure and exposure to threats.
