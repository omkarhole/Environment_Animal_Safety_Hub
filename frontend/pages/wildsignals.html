Master Communicators of the Animal Kingdom

Discover how different species have evolved sophisticated communication systems

Dolphins: Echo-Location Masters

Frequency Range 0.25 - 150 kHz
Click Rate Up to 600/second
Signature Whistles Unique Names

Dolphins use complex click sequences for echolocation and signature whistles that function like names. They can recognize each other's whistles after 20+ years of separation.

Honey Bees: The Waggle Dance

Dance Duration 15-120 seconds
Information Encoded Distance & Direction
Precision ±10° Accuracy

Honey bees perform a "waggle dance" to communicate the location of food sources. The dance's angle relative to gravity indicates direction, while duration indicates distance.

Elephants: Infrasound Specialists

Frequency 14-35 Hz (Infrasound)
Range Up to 10 km
Seismic Detection Through Feet

Elephants communicate through infrasound below human hearing range. These low-frequency sounds travel through air and ground, allowing communication over vast distances.

The Silent Language of Plants

Discover how plants communicate through chemical signals, root networks, and light responses

The Wood Wide Web

Plants communicate through mycorrhizal networks - fungal connections between root systems that allow them to share nutrients, water, and warning signals.

Distress Signals

When attacked by herbivores, plants release chemicals that warn neighboring plants to produce defensive compounds.

Resource Sharing

Mature trees share nutrients with seedlings through fungal networks, especially in low-light conditions.

Chemical Defenses

Plants produce tannins, alkaloids, and other compounds in response to communication from threatened neighbors.
