# Endocrine Disruption from Agricultural Pesticides - Feature Documentation

## Issue #1480: Endocrine Disruption from Agricultural Pesticides

### Overview
This educational feature explores how chronic exposure to modern pesticides interferes with hormonal systems in amphibians, birds, and mammals, leading to developmental abnormalities. The page provides comprehensive information about endocrine-disrupting chemicals (EDCs) and their impacts on wildlife reproduction and development.

### Features Implemented

#### 1. **Educational Content Structure**
- Hero section introducing endocrine disruption
- Overview of the EDC crisis with key statistics
- Detailed mechanisms of hormone disruption
- Species-specific impact sections for amphibians, birds, and mammals

#### 2. **Mechanisms of Disruption**
- Hormone mimics that trigger false signals
- Hormone blockers that prevent normal function
- Critical timing during developmental stages
- Low-dose chronic exposure effects

#### 3. **Species-Specific Impacts**
- **Amphibians**: Sexual abnormalities, reproductive failure, deformities
- **Birds**: Eggshell thinning, behavioral changes, population declines
- **Mammals**: Reproductive disorders, developmental toxicity, immune suppression

#### 4. **Case Studies**
- Atrazine and amphibian hermaphroditism
- DDT and bird population crashes
- Glyphosate and mammalian reproduction effects

#### 5. **Solutions and Prevention**
- Regulatory action and stronger pesticide testing
- Sustainable agriculture practices
- Research and monitoring initiatives

#### 6. **Interactive Elements**
- Animated statistics counters
- Scroll-triggered animations
- Card hover effects
- Species section slide-in animations
