# Decline of Scavenger Species and Disease Risk - Feature Documentation

## Issue #1559: Decline of Scavenger Species and Disease Risk

### Overview
This feature implements an educational page analyzing how the loss of vultures and other scavengers increases carcass persistence and pathogen spread. The page provides comprehensive information about ecological impacts, disease transmission risks, real-world case studies, and actionable conservation strategies.

### Features Implemented

#### 1. **Educational Content Structure**
- **Hero Section**: Engaging introduction with key statistics about scavenger decline
- **Ecological Impact Analysis**: Detailed explanation of carcass persistence and trophic cascades
- **Disease Transmission Risks**: Pathogen amplification mechanisms and specific disease concerns
- **Case Studies**: Real-world examples from Indian subcontinent, Africa, and Europe
- **Mitigation Strategies**: Conservation actions and alternative carcass management
- **Call-to-Action**: Interactive buttons linking to conservation resources

#### 2. **Interactive Elements**
- **Impact Statistics Grid**: Visual representation of scavenger population declines and disease risks
- **Risk Factor Cards**: Key mechanisms of pathogen spread due to scavenger loss
- **Case Study Cards**: Detailed regional examples with causes and consequences
- **Solution Grid**: Conservation and management approaches
- **Action Buttons**: Direct links to vulture conservation organizations and research resources

#### 3. **Visual Design**
- **Color-coded Sections**: Consistent theming with project design system
- **Icon Integration**: Font Awesome icons for visual hierarchy (vulture, carcass, pathogen symbols)
- **Responsive Layout**: Mobile-first design with flexible grid systems
- **Interactive Animations**: Hover effects and smooth transitions

#### 4. **Content Areas**

##### The Scavenger Crisis
- Statistics: 95-99% vulture population decline in South Asia
- Economic impact: Millions in livestock losses annually
- Global health significance for zoonotic disease prevention

##### Carcass Persistence Mechanisms
- **Extended Decomposition**: Dead animal remains persist longer without scavengers
- **Nutrient Cycling Disruption**: Slowed natural breakdown processes
- **Vector Attraction**: Increased presence of disease-carrying insects and rodents
- **Pathogen Amplification**: Bacterial and viral proliferation in persistent carcasses

##### Disease Transmission Pathways
- **Anthrax**: Spore survival in accumulated carcasses
- **Rabies**: Spread through infected carcass consumption by carnivores
- **Avian Influenza**: Poultry carcass persistence and virus mutation risks
- **Other Pathogens**: Botulism, salmonella, and emerging diseases

##### Regional Case Studies
- **Indian Subcontinent**: Diclofenac poisoning leading to vulture extinction
- **Africa**: Multiple threats causing continental vulture crisis
- **Europe**: Infrastructure and persecution impacts on scavenger species

##### Conservation Solutions
- Vulture Safe Zones
- Toxicant Regulation
- Habitat Protection
- Anti-poisoning Campaigns
- Alternative Carcass Management
- Community-based Conservation

##### Prevention Strategies
- Population Monitoring
- Disease Surveillance
- Safe Carcass Disposal
- Veterinary Drug Regulation
