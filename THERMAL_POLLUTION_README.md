# Thermal Pollution from Power Plants - Issue #1569

## Overview

This educational feature explores the ecological impacts of thermal pollution from power plant cooling systems on aquatic ecosystems. Thermal pollution occurs when warm water discharge from industrial cooling processes alters natural water temperatures, affecting aquatic metabolism, oxygen levels, and species composition.

## Issue Reference
- **GitHub Issue**: #1569
- **Title**: Thermal Pollution from Power Plants
- **Description**: Investigate how warm water discharge alters aquatic metabolism, oxygen levels, and species composition

## Educational Objectives

1. **Understand Thermal Pollution Mechanisms**
   - Learn how power plant cooling systems discharge warm water
   - Understand heat transfer processes in aquatic environments
   - Recognize temperature thresholds for aquatic organisms

2. **Explore Aquatic Ecosystem Impacts**
   - Examine effects on dissolved oxygen levels
   - Study changes in metabolic rates and respiration
   - Analyze shifts in species composition and biodiversity

3. **Investigate Biological Consequences**
   - Review impacts on fish reproduction and development
   - Understand effects on aquatic food webs
   - Explore disease susceptibility changes

4. **Examine Mitigation Strategies**
   - Learn about cooling system alternatives
   - Understand regulatory frameworks
   - Explore technological solutions
